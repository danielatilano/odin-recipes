Tequeños

Description

Tequeños are Venezuela’s favorite snack, consisting of salty white cheese spears wrapped in a thin, spiraled wheat dough and deep-fried until golden. They offer a perfect contrast between a crunchy, flaky crust and a warm, gooey, melted cheese center.

An essential at every celebration—leading to the saying "a party without tequeños isn't a party"—they are versatile enough for breakfast or a quick treat. While traditionally savory, they are often paired with guasacaca or sweet dipping sauces for the ultimate bite.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose wheat flour.
  • 5 tablespoons of cold butter (cut into small cubes).
  • 1 egg.
  • ½ teaspoon of salt.
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar (for that classic slightly sweet contrast).
  • 4 to 5 tablespoons of cold water (added gradually).
  • 300g to 400g of "Queso Blanco Duro" or "Llanero" (a firm, salty white cheese that holds its shape when heated).

Steps for preparation:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and sugar. Add the cold butter and use your fingers to rub it into the flour until it looks like coarse sand. Add the egg and the water (one tablespoon at a time).
  2. Knead the dough for about 5 minutes until it is smooth and elastic. Wrap it in plastic and let it rest at room temperature for 20 to 30 minutes. This relaxes the gluten and makes it much easier to stretch.
  3. On a floured surface, roll the dough with a rolling pin until it is very thin (about 2 mm). Cut the dough into long, even strips roughly 2 cm wide.
  4. Take a cheese stick and place the end of a dough strip at one tip.
    • Seal the end: Wrap the dough over the tip first.
    • The Spiral: Wrap the strip around the cheese stick in a spiral, overlapping the edges of the dough as you go.
    • Seal the other end: Ensure the other tip is completely covered and pinch the dough shut.
  5. Gently roll the wrapped tequeño between your palms or on the table to smooth out the layers and ensure the dough is tightly adhered to the cheese.
  6. Heat about 3 inches of oil in a pan to 175°C (350°F). Fry the tequeños in batches for 3 to 4 minutes, turning them occasionally until they are a beautiful golden brown all over.
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve them hot so the cheese is still gooey and melted inside.
