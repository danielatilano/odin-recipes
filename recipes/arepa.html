Arepa


Description

An arepa is a versatile, golden cornmeal patty that is a staple of Venezuelan cuisine. Traditionally grilled on a flat griddle, it features a satisfyingly crisp outer shell and a soft, steaming interior.

Sliced open like a pocket, it can be stuffed with endless fillings, from simple cheese and butter to savory meats or avocado. It is a beloved cultural icon, enjoyed as a hearty meal or a quick snack at any time of day.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of precooked corn flour (white or yellow)
  • 2 ½ cups of water (warm water helps prevent lumps)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt.
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil (to grease the pan)

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the water and salt. Stir until the salt is fully dissolved. If you want a slightly softer dough, ensure the water is lukewarm.
  2. Slowly pour the precooked corn flour into the water. Use your hand or a spoon to mix as you pour to prevent large lumps from forming.
  3. Let the mixture sit for 2 to 3 minutes to allow the flour to fully absorb the water. Then, knead the dough with your hands for about 2 minutes until it is smooth, shiny, and no longer sticks to your fingers.
  4. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll them into smooth balls (about the size of a large orange). Gently flatten each ball between your palms into a disc about 1.5 cm to 2 cm thick. Smooth out the edges with a damp finger.
  5. Heat a lightly greased skillet or budare over medium-high heat. Place the discs on the pan and cook for about 5 to 7 minutes per side. You are looking for a golden-brown crust with small toasted spots.
  6. For the best texture, transfer the seared arepas to a preheated oven at 200°C (400°F) for about 5 to 10 minutes. They are ready when they puff up slightly and sound "hollow" when you give them a light tap on the surface.
