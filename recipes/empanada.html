Empanadas


Description

Venezuelan empanadas are iconic deep-fried turnovers made from a precooked corn flour dough. Unlike other regional versions, their exterior is thin, crispy, and subtly sweet, often tinted a vibrant golden-orange with annatto.

These half-moon pockets are stuffed with diverse fillings like shredded beef, salty white cheese, or the hearty pabellón (beans, meat, and plantains). Best enjoyed hot, they are a staple street food usually paired with a spicy garlic sauce or creamy guasacaca.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of precooked corn flour (white or yellow).
  • 2 ½ cups of water.
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar or papelón (adds the signature slight sweetness).
  • 1 teaspoon of salt.
  • 1 teaspoon of onoto powder or oil (for the vibrant golden color).

Steps for preparation:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the water, salt, sugar, and annatto. Slowly add the corn flour while mixing with your hand. Knead for about 2 minutes until the dough is smooth, bright yellow, and has no lumps.
  2. Lay a clean piece of plastic (like a cut-open freezer bag or plastic wrap) on a flat surface. Lightly wipe the plastic with a drop of oil or water so the dough doesn't stick.
  3. Place a small ball of dough (about the size of a lemon) in the center of the plastic. Fold the plastic over the ball and use your hand or a small plate to press it down into a thin, even circle (about 3mm to 5mm thick).
  4. Open the plastic so the dough circle is exposed. Place about one tablespoon of filling in the center. Lift the bottom half of the plastic to fold the dough over the filling, creating a half-moon shape.
  5. With the plastic still covering the dough, use the rim of a circular bowl or a large glass to press down and "cut" the edges. This seals the empanada perfectly and trims away excess dough. Remove the plastic and the excess dough scraps.
  6. Heat at least 2 – 3 inches of vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot to roughly 180°C (350°F). Carefully slide the empanadas into the hot oil. Fry for about 3 to 4 minutes per side until they are golden-orange and very crispy.
  7. Remove them with a slotted spoon and place them on paper towels to drain. Let them sit for a minute—the filling inside will be extremely hot!
