Cachapas

Description

The cachapa is a thick, golden pancake made from freshly ground sweet corn kernels, giving it a rustic texture and a naturally sweet flavor. Griddled until caramelized on the outside and tender within, it is a staple of Venezuelan countryside cuisine.

It is most famously served folded over a thick slice of queso de mano, a soft, salty hand-stretched cheese. Finished with a dollop of butter or tangy cream, the dish offers a perfect, indulgent contrast between the sweetness of the corn and the saltiness of the cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 3 to 4 cups of fresh corn kernels (roughly 4–6 ears of corn).
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons of sugar (depending on how sweet the corn is).
  • ½ teaspoon of salt.
  • ¼ cup of milk or heavy cream (only if the batter is too thick).
  • 1 tablespoon of melted butter (optional, for the batter).

Steps for preparation:

  1. If using fresh ears of corn, shave the kernels off with a knife. Place the kernels in a blender or food processor. Pulse until you have a thick, chunky puree.
  2. Transfer the corn puree to a bowl. Stir in the sugar and salt. If the mixture feels too dry or thick to pour, add the milk one tablespoon at a time. The batter should be thick enough to hold its shape on a spoon but loose enough to spread slightly on a griddle.
  3. Heat a flat skillet or budare over medium-low heat. Grease it lightly with butter or oil. Because of the sugar content in the corn, cachapas burn easily, so low and slow is the secret.
  4. Pour about ½ cup of the batter onto the center of the pan. Use the back of a spoon to spread it into a circle (about 1 cm thick). Cook for 4 to 5 minutes until the edges look dry and bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  5. Once the bottom is deep golden brown and firm enough to move, carefully flip it with a large spatula. Cook the other side for another 3 to 4 minutes.
  6. While the cachapa is still on the griddle, spread a generous amount of butter on top and place a thick slice of queso de mano (or your chosen cheese) on one half.
  7. Fold the cachapa in half over the cheese. Let it sit for 30 seconds until the cheese begins to sweat and melt. Remove from the heat, add a little more butter on the outside, and serve immediately while hot and juicy.
